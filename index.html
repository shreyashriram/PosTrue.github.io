Publications
Poster Abstract: Sedentary Posture Muscle Monitoring via Active Vibratory Sensing

2022 21st ACM/IEEE International Conference on Information Processing in Sensor Networks (IPSN)

We present a sedentary posture muscle monitoring system via active vibration sensing. The system uses accelerometers to sense motor vibration propagating through muscle and collaboratively estimate the muscle activation condition. The system focuses on two challenges, including 1) sensor indirectly contacting targeted muscle (high noise level), and 2) muscles related to sedentary posture are often weak (low signal level), which leads to low SNR for existing passive sensing.

FinePose: Fine-Grained Postural Muscle Profiling via Haptic Vibration Signals

BodySys '22: Proceedings of the 2022 Workshop on Body-centric Computing Systems

We present FinePose, a system that measures postural muscle’s (trapezius) stiffness levels. FinePose repurposes the haptic vibration on the existing smart posture brace, and conducts active vibration sensing to measure the muscle elasticity. It uses an array of accelerometers to capture response and establishes a regression model to predict the muscle stiffness level. We conduct experiments, and demonstrate its feasibility.
